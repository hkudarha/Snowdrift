Manali Leh Bike

Highlights

Leh is the largest town and capital of the Ladakh region in India. Located at an altitude of 3,524 meters, it is known for its stunning natural beauty, ancient Buddhist monasteries, and unique culture. The town is surrounded by snow-capped mountains and is a popular destination for adventure tourism, with opportunities for trekking, mountaineering, river rafting, and skiing. Visitors to Leh can explore the historic Leh Palace, built in the 17th century, as well as the Namgyal Tsemo Gompa, Shanti Stupa, and the Spituk Monastery. The town also hosts a number of colorful festivals throughout the year, including the Hemis Festival, which celebrates the birth of Guru Padmasambhava, the founder of Tibetan Buddhism. With its rich history, breathtaking landscapes, and vibrant culture, Leh is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to northern India.

Tour Itinerary

Day 1: Manali

  • Arrive in Manali and check-in at Hotel
  • Rest and spend some time enjoying the beauty of nature
  • Attend orientation process and meet bike expert
  • Take a city tour by motorbike and get familiar with the bikes
  • Overnight stay at hotel

Day 2: Manali - Jispa (93 Kms)

  • After breakfast, meet tour guide for safari briefing
  • Ride your bike towards Jispa, passing Solang Valley, Atal tunnel, Tandi, and Keylong
  • Stay overnight in hotel/camps and have dinner

Day 3: Jispa - Sarchu (78 Kms)

  • After breakfast, depart towards Sarchu
  • Ride over the scenic Baralacha Pass(4850M) with plenty of stops to capture the scenic grandeur
  • After crossing the pass, drop down to the vast Sarchu plains
  • Have dinner and overnight in tents

Day 4: Sarchu - Leh (250 kms)

  • After breakfast, start journey towards Leh
  • Climb a series of Gatta loops consisting of 32 hairpin bends
  • Stop at places like Nakee La, Lachung La, Pang valley, and Tanglang La
  • Ride to Leh town and stay overnight at hotel

Day 5: Leh - Shey - Thiksey - Hemis

  • Go on a sightseeing trip to ancient monasteries in the area
  • Shey Gompa is about 15 kms from Leh, used to be summer palace of the kings of Ladakh
  • Main attraction in Shey is the 12m Shakyamuni Buddha statue
  • Thiksey Gompa, close to Shey is about 17 kms from Leh and is considered one of the most beautiful in Ladakh
  • Hemis, about 45 kms south of Leh, is one of the most famous and largest monastery in Ladakh
  • Overnight stay at the Hotel

Day 6: Leh - Nubra Valley (160 Kms)

  • After breakfast in the hotel, cross Khardung La (5602m) to enter Nubra Valley
  • Experience the white sand dunes in Nubra and stay overnight at the Hotel/Camp in Nubra Valley

Day 7: Nubra to Leh (160 Kms)

  • After an early breakfast, we can visit the white sand dunes at Hunder and enjoy a ride of the double humped Bactrian camels exclusively found in Ladakh.
  • We drive back to Leh - our base.
  • Overnight in the hotel.

Day 8: Leh - Pangong Tso (154 Kms one-way)

  • A trip to the worlds highest Lake Pangong.
  • The lake is a major attraction of the region and is amongst the highest altitude saline water lakes in the world and changes colors from shades of blue to green to red.
  • Featured in movies like 3-idiots and Jab Tak Hai Jaan, it is said to be the most beautiful lake in India.
  • Return back to Leh.
  • Overnight in the hotel.

Day 9: Leh - Sarchu(250Kms)

  • After a healthy breakfast, check out from the hotel of Leh.
  • Today is the day to say goodbye to the land of mysterious and proceed to Sarchu (4290 m).
  • Reach the destination by evening and enjoy your stay at Sarchu Camps, have dinner and stay overnight at the camp in Sarchu.

Day 10: Sarchu - Manali - Delhi Departure Overnight Journey

  • Post breakfast check out from the hotel/Camp of Sarchu.
  • Today you will be going to Manali.
  • By evening you will be taking a Volvo Bus for escorting back to Delhi for your onward journey.
