Chandrakhani Trek

Highlights

Chandrakhani Pass Trek is a popular high-altitude trekking trail situated in the Kullu Valley of Himachal Pradesh, India. This moderate to difficult trek is about 26 km long and can take up to 5 days to complete. The trekking trail takes you through a beautiful and diverse landscape that ranges from lush green forests to rugged mountain peaks. The highlight of the trek is the stunning view of the snow-clad peaks of Deo Tibba, Pir Panjal and Parbati from the top of the Chandrakhani Pass.

Chandrakhani Pass Trek is an excellent option for adventure seekers who want to experience the beauty of the Himalayas without going on an extremely challenging trek. The best time to go on this trek is from May to October, when the weather is pleasant and the trails are clear. The trek can be completed by people of different fitness levels, but it is recommended to do some physical training before starting the trek to avoid altitude sickness and other health issues.

Tour Itinerary

Day 1: Drive from Manali to Rumsu. Trek from Rumsu to Naya Tapru

  • Drive from Manali to Rumsu.
  • Trek from Rumsu to Naya Tapru.
  • Drive Distance: 28 km | Drive Duration: 1 hour | Trek Distance: 6 km | Trek Duration: 6 hours | Altitude Gain: 6,745 ft to 10,430 ft

Day 2: Trek from Naya Tapru to Chaklani

  • Trek from Naya Tapru to Chaklani.
  • Trek Distance: 2.5 km | Trek Duration: 2.5 hours | Altitude Gain: 10,430 ft to 11,368 ft

Day 3: Trek from Chaklani to Nagruni via Chandrakhani Pass

  • Trek from Chaklani to Nagruni via Chandrakhani Pass.
  • Trek Distance: 9.2 km | Trek Duration: 9 hours | Altitude Gain/Loss: 11,368 ft to 12,185 ft and back down to 11,145 ft.

Day 4: Trek from Nagruni to Malana via Magic valley.

  • Trek from Nagruni to Malana via Magic valley.
  • Drive back to Manali.
  • Trek Distance: 3.6 km | Trek Duration: 3 hours | Altitude Loss: 11,145 ft to 8,260 ft

Important Point

  • You will be staying in tents on all days of the trek
  • The distance between campsites may vary by 100 meters depending on weather conditions and route taken
  • Altitude may vary by 100 feet for similar reasons
  • Trekkers must carry a copy of their photo ID for entry at forest check posts on the trek.
